Accessichat — Honourable Mention in Augmented Creativity

Accessichat is an augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) application designed to improve verbal and written communication for individuals with visible and invisible disabilities. The app leverages AI to provide accessible communication tools, helping users achieve greater independence and confidence in their daily interactions.

Product Development

PROBLEM SPACE

This project was part of the 2023 Remarkable Designathon, where we were tasked to explore: How might we use AI to enable greater independence and participation for people with disabilities in their daily lives and communities?

WORKING WITH OUR USERS

Throughout this project, we closely collaborated with 3 individuals with disabilities who provided invaluable insights across all stages, from initial user interviews to multiple rounds of usability testing and ongoing consultation. These participants were compensated for their time and contributions.

Our Participant Selection Process

We began by reaching out to the disability community and distributing a pre-screening survey via Google Forms.

This survey served two key purposes:
To evaluate if participants' experiences aligned with our problem statement
To ensure ethical participation guidelines were met

In the process, we intentionally sought diversity in our participant group across age, gender, and disability. Our focus was primarily on individuals with neurological and physical disabilities who often face challenges in communication, encounter societal biases, and struggle to find effective tools for self-expression.

Fig 4. Our Empathy Maps based on 3 Users

They identified with several key pain points and considerations:

Communication Barriers

3 users lacked tools for immediate or effective communication

Social Stigma

2 in 3 users had limited safe spaces to express needs without fear of judgment

Daily Socialization

2 in 3 users faced difficulty socializing due to societal biases and overwhelming environments

Helpful Tools

2 in 3 users found value in text and audio outputs for processing information and communicating

Discrimination

1 in 3 users lacked accessible information on addressing bias and discrimination

Competitive Analysis

We conducted a competitive analysis focusing on tools and applications our participants actively used, such as Proloquo2go, Otter.ai, and Finch. By examining these familiar applications, we gained valuable insights into successful user experience elements and performance metrics.

TECHNOLOGY & CAPACITIES

Our analysis revealed key success criteria across these applications:

While apps like Finch and Proloquo2go offer manual customization settings, AI-powered apps excel in creating adaptive, personalized experiences that evolve with users over time.

Leading apps utilize Natural Language Processing (NLP), Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR), and generative AI for features like real-time transcription, speech-to-text conversion, and personalized recommendations.

All successful tools prioritize their users' communication goals, whether supporting those with speech impairments or providing mental health assistance.

Problem Reframing

We synthesized our research into three key "How Might We" (HMW) statements:

HMW increase tools for immediate and effective communication for people with disabilities?

HMW create opportunities for people with disabilities to express their needs without fear or stigma?

HMW enhance daily experiences for people with disabilities to help them manage life more effectively and enjoyably?

This process allowed us to come to an appropriate and narrow, redefined problem statement.

The Problem Statement

Individuals with neurological and physical disabilities need effective accommodations to communicate clearly and concisely. These tools will empower them to manage their daily experiences in a more effective and enjoyable way.

The Solution

ACCESSICHAT'S VALUE

Accessichat is an accommodating app dedicated to enhancing communication for individuals with disabilities. We worked to include features that would expand the means that people with disabilities can communicate through advanced features such as:

Speech-to-Text Conversion for real-time communication

Scenario Practice for preparing for real-life conversations

Personalized Profiles with customizable "About Me" sections

Customizable Avatars reflecting cultural and personal preferences

Goal Setting and Progress tracking, allowing people to meet their communication goals and win in-app awards

WHAT SETS US APART

Our app combines advanced communication features with tools for emotional well-being and empowerment. It recognizes unique speech patterns, supports note-sharing, and incorporates mental health features and positive affirmations. By introducing an award system to celebrate achievements and expanding communication options for people with disabilities, our app stands out as an inclusive and empowering platform.

Usability Testing

Our 3 participants each engaged in 2 rounds of testing, with each session lasting 20 minutes over Zoom. During each session, participants shared their screens and cameras while exploring the core features of the app. We evaluated both their ease of using the app and asked specific questions derived from our Assumptions Map to validate or challenge our initial assumptions.

Final Outcomes

The project achieved significant improvements by incorporating insights from user testing, ensuring the final product addressed their needs. Furthermore, accessible communication tools were successfully integrated with AI-driven features, enhancing functionality for users with all kinds of disabilities. The consistent user group confirmed their willingness to adopt the application if made available. This validation, coupled with the app's focus on augmented creativity, earned it an Honorable Mention at the 2024 RGD Student Design Awards.

Key Learning & Next Steps

The project provided valuable insights into AI integration, demonstrating how generative AI can be leveraged for competitive analysis and feature development without compromising accessibility. Regular user testing played a pivotal role, ensuring cultural alignment and informed design decisions. Lastly, the adoption of agile methodology allowed the team to continually improve the product based on user feedback, resulting in a more refined and user-centered solution. Moving forward, these lessons will guide the team in creating even more impactful and inclusive tools.

* Feedback from RGD highlighted areas for improvement, particularly in addressing WCAG guidelines and enhancing color contrast requirements. Given more time, these aspects could have been further refined to elevate the product’s accessibility standards.
