Product Development

PROBLEM SPACE

As university students ourselves, my team identified mental health as a prevalent challenge among peers. We wanted to create a tool that facilitates mindfulness and emotional awareness through journaling.

USER INTERVIEWS

We started off by interviewing 8 students from York University and Sheridan College who use or considered journaling as a self-reflection method.

Insights were also sought from a psychotherapist for information on navigating sensitive topics responsibly while designing a mental health focused product.

To ensure we approached the topic of mental health thoughtfully:

Interviews were conducted over Zoom for a week, each session lasting 30 minutes each.

I was involved in developing a script with open-ended questions to explore participants' habits, challenges, and motivations.

By hearing from a variety of interviewees, we could learn from different perspectives and their varying pain points.

Our Curiosities

In total, we prepared 17 questions for the students, split into two segments.

Some questions we prepared for the students included:

// Methods and outlets

How do you keep track of or document your daily experiences and feelings? Can you walk me through that process?

What tools or other outlets do you utilise in reflecting your emotions? (friends, families, notes, social media?)

// Ideals and painpoints

What does your ideal reflection/coping method look like?

Are there any unique features in your method of documentation that you like/dislike?

Do you think self-reflecting will help your mental health?

What People are saying

How do you keep track of or document your daily experiences and feelings? Can you walk me through that process?

“I meditate at night, if I don't pass out from exhaustion. I don’t formally document anything or follow a routine, but I try to stay aware of my mood choices.”–C.A.

What could be better about your current experience?

"Identifying my emotions can be so hard sometimes. It'd be nice to have a way to just list everything out on a blank page and have it sorted for me as a starting point—like putting everything up on a wall without needing to organize it myself, or better yet, having it already organized for me." –T.N.

Do you actively seek a solution to your problem? Or go with the flow?

“ I mostly go with the flow. I am more reactive than proactive.” –F.B.

So,,, what now?

Through a prioritization grid, we brainstormed potential solutions, considering both Big Bets (aka high-risk, high-reward ideas) and No-Brainers (simple, high-impact solutions). After discussing the options, we voted on the top 3 priorities that would deliver the most value. This process led to a clear problem statement that focused on addressing the primary painpoints our user had

HIS STORY

Patrick is a 25 year old law school student. Patrick undergoes a lot of stress during his day while working on assignments as he does not have enough time to reflect on stressful situations. He goes to the gym to deal with a lot of his stress, but sometimes feels like it is not enough to alleviate his stress completely.
