Product Development

PROBLEM SPACE

As university students ourselves, my team identified mental health as a prevalent challenge among peers. We wanted to create a tool that facilitates mindfulness and emotional awareness through journaling.

USER INTERVIEWS

We started off by interviewing 8 students from York University and Sheridan College who use or considered journaling as a self-reflection method.

To ensure we approached the topic of mental health thoughtfully:

Interviews were conducted over Zoom for a week, each session lasting 30 minutes each.
I developed a script with open-ended questions to explore participants' habits, challenges, and motivations.

By hearing from a variety of interviewees, we could learn from different perspectives and their varying pain points.

What People are saying

Our Curiosities

In total, we prepared 17 questions, split into (1) their methods and outlets of self-reflection (2) their ideals and painpoints.

Some questions we prepared included:

METHODS AND OUTLETS

How do you keep track of or document your daily experiences and feelings? Can you walk me through that process?

What tools or other outlets do you utilise in reflecting your emotions? (friends, families, notes, social media?)

IDEALS AND PAINPOINTS

What does your ideal reflection/coping method look like?

Are there any unique features in your method of documentation that you like/dislike?

Do you think self-reflecting will help your mental health?
