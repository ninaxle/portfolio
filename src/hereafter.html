Product Development

PROBLEM SPACE

As university students ourselves, my team identified mental health as a prevalent challenge among peers. We wanted to create a tool that facilitates mindfulness and emotional awareness through journaling.

USER INTERVIEWS

We started off by interviewing 8 students from York University and Sheridan College who use or considered journaling as a self-reflection method.

To ensure we approached the topic of mental health thoughtfully:

Interviews were conducted over Zoom for a week, each session lasting 30 minutes each.
I developed a script with open-ended questions to explore participants' habits, challenges, and motivations.

By hearing from a variety of interviewees, we could learn from different perspectives and their varying pain points.

Problem Statement

How might we create a simple and convenient journaling app to help students consistently reflect and improve their emotional well-being?

Poor Mental Health: Limited awareness of affordable resources.

Motivation: Users struggle to prioritize mental health.

Routine: Inconsistent journaling habits.

Convenience: Lack of a quick, easy journaling method.

Anxiety: Uncertainty about managing stress effectively.

Openness: Hesitation to confide due to fear of judgment.

So, what now?

Shirley feels like they would like to start journalling more regularly. They already self-document irregularly and they want to find ways to do it more efficiently so it does not feel stressful or like a chore. They want occasional reminders so they do not push it back but also have the options to stop those reminders when it becomes stressful.
